Abra

The Land of the Itnegs

What is Abra?

Abra is a landlocked province found in the Cordillera Administrative Region. It is bordered by the provinces of Apayao, Kalinga, Mountain Province, Ilocos Sur, and Ilocos Norte. The province is mountainous but it also has the Abra and Tineg River, where most of the population lives.

During the pre-colonial period, Abra was inhabited by Bontocs, Ifugaos, and Itnegs. The name Abra came from the Spanish word "abra" which means gap or opening which refers to the Banaoang Gap where the Abra river flows through the Ilocos mountain range.

With these geography and history, let us see the natural landmarks of Abra.
  • Tayum Church in Tayum, Abra
  • Kaparkan Falls in Tineg, Abra
  • Tangadan Tunnel in San Quintin, Abra
  • Apao Rolling Hills in Tineg, Abra
  • Tugot ni Angalo in San Quintin, Abra
  • Kili Falls in Tubo, Abra
